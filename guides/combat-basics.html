Combat Basics: Winning the Outpost Fight

Updated Jun 15, 2026 · Guides Team

Most early combat losses come down to fleet composition, not fleet size. This guide covers the fundamentals our syndicate officers teach every new recruit before their first outpost contest.

Fleet Composition

  • Skirmish ships hit hard and fast but lose extended engagements — use them to punish overextended enemy fleets.
  • Siege ships excel against structures and shields but are vulnerable without escort.
  • Support ships provide the repair and buff uptime that decides close fights — never leave home without at least one.

Formation Bonuses

Formations grant stacking bonuses based on ship-type ratios. A balanced 3:2:1 skirmish-siege-support ratio is the safest general-purpose formation until you know what you're up against.

Timing an Outpost Contest

  • Scout first. Committing a fleet blind is the single most common cause of avoidable losses.
  • Coordinate arrival times with your syndicate — staggered fleets get picked off one at a time.
  • Hold a reserve. The fight after the fight is usually the one that decides who keeps the outpost.

After the Battle

Review the combat report with your syndicate. Loss or win, the report shows exactly which ship types traded well — use it to adjust your next fleet build rather than guessing.
