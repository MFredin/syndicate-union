Anyone can submit a guide for review — you don't need to be in Research & Industry. Here's what reviewers actually look for.

## Structure over length

A tightly organized 500-word guide gets accepted faster than a sprawling 2,000-word one. Reviewers favor:

- A clear one-sentence summary near the top
- Numbered priorities or steps, not long paragraphs
- A named difficulty level and target audience

## Accuracy checks

Every submission gets tested against current-season mechanics before acceptance. Guides referencing outdated numbers or a previous season's meta are sent back with specific notes, not rejected outright.

## Submission process

1. Draft your guide in the submission template (available on the Downloads page)
2. Post it in `#guide-submissions` on Discord
3. A Research & Industry reviewer will respond within one week with feedback or acceptance

Accepted authors earn the Guide Author badge and a byline on the published guide.
