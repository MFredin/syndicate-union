The Union Charter began as a shared document between five commanders who'd flown together off and on for two seasons. What started as a list of complaints about the state of the frontier slowly turned into something more deliberate: a set of principles they wanted their next syndicate to actually hold to.

They landed on five: **liberty**, **unity**, **honour**, **progress**, and **service**. Not slogans — arguments. Liberty meant no member should be pressured into a playstyle that didn't suit them. Unity meant the syndicate came before any one commander's ego, including the founders'. Honour meant keeping your word to allies even when it cost you. Progress meant building things that would still matter after the current season ended. Service meant leadership was a responsibility to the group, not a reward for founding it.

They called it Syndicate Union, and they opened the doors the same week the Charter was finished. Thirty people joined in the first month. None of the five founders expected it to still exist a year later.
