For its first year, Syndicate Union treated every neighboring syndicate the same way most of the frontier did: as a rival until proven otherwise. That changed when a smaller, struggling syndicate on the Union's border sent an unusual message — not a threat, not a trade request, but an honest offer of mutual non-aggression while both groups focused on rebuilding.

It would have been easy to ignore, or worse, to treat as an opening to expand at their expense. Instead, the Council debated it for a full week and decided the offer was consistent with everything the Charter claimed to stand for. If liberty and honour meant anything, they had to apply to outsiders too, not just to fellow Unionists.

The resulting agreement was the Union's first formal treaty, and the beginning of what would become the Diplomatic Corps. That smaller syndicate is still an ally today — one of twelve organizations SU now holds formal agreements with.
