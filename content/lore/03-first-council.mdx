By the third month, Syndicate Union had outgrown its five founders' ability to just talk everything through in one voice channel. Decisions were getting made by whoever happened to be online, and not everyone agreed those were the right decisions.

The founders could have kept control themselves. Instead, in keeping with the Charter's service principle, they put the question to the membership: how should the Union govern itself? The answer, after several long debates, was the Council of Elders — one elected representative per department, plus an elected Chancellor to chair the body and cast tie-breaking votes.

The first Council election was chaotic, good-natured, and completely nonbinding in the sense that everyone involved was making it up as they went. It also worked well enough that, seasons later, the structure remains largely unchanged.
