Syndicate Union today looks very different from the thirty-member syndicate that opened its doors with a shared document and five stubborn principles. Six departments now handle everything from fleet operations to diplomacy to the guide library. The Council of Elders still meets, still argues, and still occasionally gets it wrong before getting it right.

What hasn't changed is the Charter. Every department decision, every treaty, every onboarding session still gets measured against the same five values: liberty, unity, honour, progress, and service. It's not a perfect record — no community's is — but it's the standard the Union holds itself to, in public, on a page anyone can read.

The frontier is still fractured in plenty of places. Syndicate Union exists as a bet that it doesn't have to be everywhere.
