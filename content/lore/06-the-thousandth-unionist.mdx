excerpt: "A milestone that forced the Union to reckon with what it means to stay welcoming at scale."
---

Crossing one thousand members should have felt like an unambiguous win, and mostly it did. But it also surfaced a real worry among the founding generation: could a community this size still feel like the one they'd built at thirty members? Could a newcomer in their first week still get a real answer from a real person, not just a bot reply and a wall of unread channels?

The answer the Union settled on was to formalize what had, until then, been informal kindness. Recruitment & Onboarding became a proper department with a defined roadmap, dedicated mentors, and a standing onboarding session run every week rather than whenever someone had time. It wasn't a perfect solution, but it meant growth didn't have to come at the cost of the welcome the Charter promised.

The thousandth member was celebrated with the first Founding Day event — part genuine milestone, part excuse for the whole Union to get in one Discord call at the same time, which by then was no small feat.
